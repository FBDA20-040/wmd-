History of Bulawayo

When king Mzilikazi's son Lobengula succeeded to the throne, Bulawayo served as the capital of the Ndebele state. The original royal town of Lobengula, founded in 1872, was 14 miles south of the current capital, on a ridge occupied by the Thabas Inyoka - "hill of serpents." King Lobengula named his royal town Bulawayo meaning killing. On 4th November 1893, a tattered union Jack flag was lifted to commemorate Bulawayo's capitulation and the trimphant end of war. Bulawayo was established a city when Hary White became the head of town.
